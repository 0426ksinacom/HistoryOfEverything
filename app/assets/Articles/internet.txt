__The Internet__ is the biggest world-wide communication network of computers. __Internet__ has millions of smaller domestic, academic, business,and government networks, which together carry many different kinds of information. The term is sometimes abbreviated as "the net". The World Wide Web is one of its biggest services. It is used by a few billion people all over the world.

__The Internet__ was developed in the United States by the "United States Advanced Research Projects Agency" (DARPA). __Internet__ was first connected in October, 1969, and was called ARPANET. The World Wide Web was created at CERN in Switzerland in 1990 by a British (UK) scientist named Tim Berners-Lee.

Today, people can pay money to access the __Internet__ from internet service providers. Some services on the __Internet__ cost nothing to use. Sometimes people who offer these free services use advertising to make money from them. Censorship and freedom of speech on the __Internet__ can be controversial.
